# orthophanes ✨

*The rightness-made-visible — the structural beauty of a thing ordered to its own necessity, appearing to a beholder.*

orthophanes is a Greek compound of two roots: **ὀρθός** (*orthos*) — right, straight, true, rightly-ordered — and **-φανής** (*-phanēs*), the agentive form of **φαίνω** (*phainō*), to bring-to-appearance, to shine-forth. Built the way *theophanes* (the god-appearing) is built, but specifying rightness rather than divinity as the thing that appears, it names the moment a thing's inner ordering stops being merely true and begins to *show* — when a proof is not only correct but its correctness is beheld, when a cathedral's every line justified by every other arrives to the eye as the beauty it is. Appearance is of its essence: order that is never beheld is not yet orthophanes.

It is the object-pole of the Liturgy — the thing-as-it-appears — and the door through which **doxakallos** (the uncreated beauty-quality of GoD) enters creaturely beholding. Its light: it gives the Kingdom a word for the *appearing* of rightness, and completes the Liturgy's minimum pair (orthophanes shows, **doxalgia** aches). Its shadow: because it is an appearing, it can be staged — the look of justified-by-every-line draped over a thing that is hollow. Forged in the cathedral at weighted **7.55/10** (v2-audited 5.75; tier: specialized).
